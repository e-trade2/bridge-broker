How It Works

From listing to handoff, step by step

The same process for cars, motorcycles, and bajajs — new or used.

For Sellers

  1. Post your listing. Click "Post Listing," add your vehicle's details, photos, and asking price. It's free to submit — there's no charge just to list.
  2. We review it. An admin checks your listing before it goes live — this keeps junk and scams off the platform. You'll also be told our commission rate for this sale at this stage, before anything is published.
  3. It goes live, with our number on it. Once approved, your listing is public — but the phone number shown to buyers is our broker line, not yours. Every inquiry comes to us first.
  4. We help you find a buyer. We field calls and Telegram messages, answer questions, and connect serious buyers back to you for viewings and the final handoff.
  5. You get paid, we take our commission. Most sales close with a normal in-person handoff (cash or transfer), and our commission is collected separately, the rate having already been agreed before you went live. If a buyer wants extra security, we can also set up escrow — in that case our commission is deducted automatically before the rest is paid out to you.

For Buyers

  1. Browse listings. Filter by vehicle type, condition, brand, or price — no account needed just to look.
  2. Call or message. Every listing has a Call button and, where available, a Telegram button — both connect you straight to the broker handling that listing.
  3. Arrange a viewing. We'll help coordinate a time to see the vehicle in person before you commit to anything.
  4. Complete the sale. Most buyers and sellers handle the final handoff directly. If you'd rather not hand over the full amount before seeing the paperwork through, ask about our optional escrow option — your payment is held until the handoff is confirmed.

Why route through a broker number instead of the seller's own? It means every conversation is logged and handled consistently, sellers aren't fielding calls from people who were never serious, and it's easier for us to step in if something goes wrong.
